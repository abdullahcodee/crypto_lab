Rail Fence Cipher Explanation

The Rail Fence cipher is a transposition cipher that rearranges the plaintext characters in a zigzag pattern across multiple "rails" or lines. It was used by the ancient Greeks, but its modern version is credited to the American Civil War.

To encrypt a message using the Rail Fence cipher, the plaintext is written diagonally up and down across a number of "rails" or lines equal to the key. The ciphertext is then formed by reading off the letters in the order they appear.

Example:

Key: 3
Plain Text: HELLO WORLD
Encryption Process:

                    H . . . O . . . L . . . W . .
                    . E . L . W . R . D . . O . .
                    . . L . . . . . . . . . . . .
Encrypted Text: HO LW RD E LWRDO

To decrypt a message encrypted with the Rail Fence cipher, the same number of "rails" or lines used for encryption is drawn, and the ciphertext letters are written in the zigzag pattern. The plaintext is then read off from the diagonals.

Example:

Key: 3
Encrypted Text: HO LW RD E LWRDO
Decryption Process:

                    H . . . O . . . L . . . W . .
                    . E . L . W . R . D . . O . .
                    . . L . . . . . . . . . . . .
Plain Text: HELLO WORLD

The Rail Fence cipher is relatively simple and can be easily cracked through brute force or frequency analysis, especially for shorter messages.
